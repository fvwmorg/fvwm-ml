Nancy Davis <nedavis_at_cs.utah.edu> writes:
> > Nancy Davis <nedavis_at_cs.utah.edu> writes:
> > > Hi FVWM folks,
> > >
> > > I have recently installed fvwm2 on NetBSD. I have something
> > > misconfigured, however. I cannot see any icons within
> > > programs,
> >
> > What does "icons within programs" mean? Do you mean that you
> > can't see any icons when you minimize a program?
>
> Well, the icon of the minimized program does not display as anything
> other than a shadow on the desktop...i.e., there is no icon, just
> a space where it should be.

I'm not sure what would cause that. Sounds like you are displaying
icons, you just aren't displaying any color.

> Also icons that are displayed as part
> of the Xwindow (i.e., netscape "Back", "Home" et cetera) only show
> up as blank boxes.

Fvwm doesn't control the icons within Netscape. Sounds like a more
general problem.

> > > For example, xbiff is installed and is supposed to show up
> > > on the window manager box, but I have a blank space. Netscape
> > > fires up, but the buttons do not have any icons on them.
> >
> > What is a "window manager box"? Do you mean FvwmIconBox?
>
> When I fire up FvwmIconBox as a separate process, I only see a
> blank box.

That would be expected, if you are using desktop icons,
FvwmIconBox would have nothing to show.

> I'm not certain what the process is called upon startup,
> but I have a box that contains FvwmIconMan, a 2x2 desktop, a clock,
> some type of system monitor and supposedly xbiff. xbiff is just
> a blank space.

Sounds like you are running FvwmButtons with FvwmIconMan and some other
stuff inside it. Its not clear whether xbiff is not in the button bar
or whether its just not displaying any color.

> I am wondering if this might be related to the ability to display
> colors from my video card and monitor settings. Where might I find
> error messages of this type logged?

Its still hard to tell from what you've described.

Do you see any color on the screen at all?

What does the command "xdpyinfo" print?

I'm not familiar with the NETBSD setup. If you are using "startx",
you might try:

startx 2>&1>/tmp/errors.out

All your error message should end up in /tmp/errors.out

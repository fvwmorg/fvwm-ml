On Fri, Apr 01, 2005 at 09:30:27PM -0300, Dedeco wrote:
> I have this line on my .fvwm2rc to make my root window nice:
> + "I" Exec "fvwm-root" /path/to/image.png
>
> The image I have isn't the exact ratio of the screen (it is taller)
> and I want it to be shown centered and fully appearing. Currently is is
> stretched to the sides of the screen and top aligned with it (so the
> bottom of it isn't shown). Checking "$man fvwm-root" I found that the
> command doesn't have this option, right? How can I do then?

I second Nuno Alexamdre's recommendation of feh for this
purpose. It works very nicely with fvwm. In particular,
it handles transparent elements very well.

However I use feh directly, without appeal to fbsetbg or any
another auxiliary program. In init I have:

+ I Exec feh --bg-scale /path/to/image.png

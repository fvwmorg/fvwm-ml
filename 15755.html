OK, I will bite.... I really like fvwms' current feature list much more
then other window managers total features, however there are a number of
"missing" features that I think would be nice. Times change and creative
people come up with feature on various WMs that can improve any WM.
I used to use tvtwm, but I thought the 3d look was enough better that I
was willing to do without all the nice pixmap support for buttons and
title bars, its' nicer icon manager, and other features. I for one am very
glad to see some of that support finally starting to get added to FVWM.

I as long term Unix user have often pooh poohed Windows and such. However
I now find that I spened half my time at work using FVWM, and the other
half at home with Win95 and XWin32 doing the same things I do at work. All
except the window manager. There are some feature in Win95 that are
convienient to say the least. They have had a chance to look at what the
other WM's have done right and wrong, and have made some good decisions
that would be nice to see on other window managers. (Thanks God there is a
public domain Win95 utility that turns off click to type.... )

I am not speaking "emulation per say", but Win95 does pixmaps well, and
their icon manager is very appealing. I also really like to click on a
"window" and have it come to the foreground. This is alot more straight
forward then trying place my pointer on a thin "window border" like I do
with FVWM. The way it deals with color maps to "keep from running out" is
also slick.

I know this is creaping featureism, and not something that Rob Nation
wanted to do himself, but as long as some of these features can pass
the bloat test, or be confined to modules, I would like to see FVWM
continue to "grow" and continue as "the best" and "my favorite" WM.

On Mon, 23 Jun 1997, Jeremy Dinsel wrote:

: For instance, I wish to take a window in screen A and move it to
: screen B (to clear up clutter).. I drag the window and let go of the
: moose button while it is still moving..moments later, it slows down
: and stops (as any ordinary element would when obstructed by
: friction).

This is a neat idea. There are times when a window is in my way, and
I want it somewhere else - over there to the right, say - but I don't
really care exactly where. I click, I toss, it moves and glides to
halt as I bring into focus the window underneath.

Gives a new meaning to "throwing an xterm," too.

Pete Forman wrote:
> Neil Bird writes:
> > Why doesn't the FVWM mailing list have itself as the Reply-To? I
> > have to keep changing the 'To' to prevent myself from emailing the
> > missive to the original author!
>
> I raised that question three weeks ago on this list. The bottom line
> is that Reply-To _should_ go to the author alone. Followup should be
> used to reply to the list.

  Actually, I've partially followed a big thread on this wrt. the home
mailer I use. I don't recall there being a proper consensus: someone said
that 'Reply-To:' should be the 'personal reply' bit (i.e., to the author)
and the 'From:' the mailing-list ... but then someone else pointed to:

... the 'Majordomo FAQ'
<URL:http://www.cis.ohio-state.edu/~barr/majordomo-faq.html>
and the 'Mailing List Management Software FAQ'
<URL:ftp://ftp.uu.net/usenet/news.answers/mail/list-admin/software-faq>
for reference.

  Which, to quote his summary, say:

 - Replies should be sent to Reply-To: field if present (mailing list
   software should set this to point to the 'posting address')
 - The Author's Email address should always be shown in the From:
   field (if the Reply-To: field isn't set, this address should be
   used)
 - The actual poster (if the author is using another machine) should
   have the address shown in Sender: (if not present, assume From:)
 - Errors-To: /may/ be set to the administrator of the mailing list
   (if applicable) or the administrator of the sending machine.
   Mailer-Daemon software should send failure messages et al to this
   address.

  Either way, shouldn't we have /both/ a 'From:' and 'Reply-To:',
whichever way around they go?

Bob Woodside wrote:
> jason wrote:
> >
> > The problem: sometimes, if I try to move my mouse pointer into another
> > page, I get "blocked" on the edges by the Netscape window. I just can't
> > "push" past the border of the window. It's not always the same edge -- it
> > can be the left, right, or bottom edge (never the top edge, since this
> > only occurs for Netscape borders against the edge of the current page).
>
> ...and Bob wrote:
> >
> > The window doesn't have to be maxed out, it just needs to have the
> > top border jammed up against the top of the viewport.
>
> Reading back over the entire thread, it looks as though the key
> condition is that *any* border of the offending Motif window is at the
> edge of the viewport where you want to cross to another page. It
> definitely will occur at the top edge as well, and that's the only place
> I remembered seeing it happen.
>
>
> - Bob

In the past I have described this to other fvwm2 users and they
have always thought I was crazy. I have never been able to
reproduce it on demand, and so far only with Netscape ( I don't
think I use any other motif apps ).

I see the same thing on Linux with XFree86 and on Solaris
with openwin. It will only happen if netscape is "busy" at
the time, and only when I try to automaticaly cross a border
that has the edge of Netscape jammed against it.

For the past year or so I have just changed the way I use
netscape - I use it in the bottom left screen of a single 3x3
desktop and make the window about 95% fullscreen so only the
left edge touches the screen edge. I don't have anything to
cross there, so it hides the problem.

On Tue, 10 Jul 2001, Dominik Vogt wrote:

> On Mon, Oct 01, 2001 at 10:08:27PM -0400, Kendrick Vargas wrote:
> > Is there any chance.. or... how difficult would it be to turn the pager
> > into a Gnome Applet?
>
> I don't know the Gnome specs for applets, so I can't really answer
> your question. The EWMH spec includes some rules for pager
> applications to communicate with desktop environments and this
> will eventually be implemented in fvwm. If your question is only
> about swallowing the pager in some gnome specific panel or tool
> bar, then it's probably quite easy to do - but keep in mind that
> we don't want to introduce new dependencies with third party
> libraries without good reason.

Actually, what I meant was running the FVWM Pager outside of FVWM, so that
if I chose I could use it with other window managers. I love using FVWM at
work because it allows me to be extremely productive. However at home I
don't really care what window manager I use, and gnome isn't all that bad,
except that the only pager they have sucks more than a team of hoovers on
"high". I haven't seen a pager as configurable and useful as the one from
FVWM. So I was wondering if it'd be difficult to take the FvwmPager out of
fvwm and generalize it for something like Gnome.

PS> I have had fvwm running inside gnome for a while at my office. At home
I've been using Nautilus and other stuff for filemanagement, and some of
them don't play nice with fvwm...

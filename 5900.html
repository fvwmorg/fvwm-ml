On 16 Aug 2002 13:27:50 -0600, Gregg Dameron wrote:
>
> Mikhael Goikhman wrote:
>
> > + I ThisWindow (!Iconic Raised) + I Iconify on
> > + I ThisWindow (!Iconic !Raised) + I Focus
> > + I ThisWindow (!Iconic !Raised) + I Raise
>
> Does "Raised" mean the window is not iconified _and_ has focus _and_ is
> not partially or fully obscured by other windows?

No, Raised only means not obscured by other windows. There may be several
such windows on the desktop. It's irrelative of iconic or focused.

Most of icons (if they are not obscured) would match the Raised condition.
So if there are normal icons (as opposed to NoIcon) a window may be in
2x2x2 = 8 states regarding the Iconic, Raised and Focused conditions. If
NoIcon is used, I think, Raised is always true when Iconic, so there are
6 states.

> The man page ("Current" command) is silent about this condition.

Yup.

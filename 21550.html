And Brandon Waldo spake, saying:
> Wesley Hart boldly stated:
> > > Do you keep a .fvwm2.log file ?
> >
> > I hadn't been keeping a log file, but on your advice I now do.
>
> How do you get fvwm to generate a log file?
>


Well, it probably depends on how you're running fvwm. I use:

     exec /home/hart/fvwm2/fvwm2 > ${HOME}/logs/fvwm.log 2>&1

as the last line of my .xsession file. If you like lots of
output and really slow performance, you can also throw -debug
in there as well. YMMV.

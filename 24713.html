PS: In case the external launching seems pointless, here's an example
where it's particularly convenient. When I'm at home, I log in to work
from my laptop, & start 8 xterms on the laptop, & do 8 rlogins to the
same host at work. This is manually tedious, especially when the line
is not all that reliable. So I have a script called 'xterms', & just say
        xterms -h chook a-da-d
where chook is the host at work, & a,b,c,d are positions on the screen.
(The script sets the xterm names so they go to the chook iconmanager, &
sets the xterm geometries.)

> Ah, sorry.. more details:

> My Sun has the CDE junk on it so it comes up with that standard login
> screen... in other words, when you reboot your Sun it comes up with a GUI
> login screen.

I don't know how this works on a Sun, but on my AIX (over a year in the past
now) you could disable the graphiical login by removing the 'dtlogin'
(or something else beginning with 'dt') from your /etc/inittab and then
starting X manually. As root you might as well 'kill' the process (use
ps to get a list of processes with names beginning with 'dt' and kill them
one by one :) ).

> You can adjust your .xinitrc to start FVWM and so that is how
> I am running FVWM on CDE (I think that login screen is part of CDE).
>
> The funny thing is, I had my FVWM working fine on another Sun w/o the CDE
> stuff and the META (diamond) key worked fine there... so I think it has
> something to do with CDE but don't know how to disable it.

Look at the output of 'xmodmap -pm'. Is there an entry mapping
mod1 to your meta key? If not you should ajust your key mappings
with the xmodmap command (see man page).

On 26 Jul 2003 11:56:49 -0600, Chris Staskewicz wrote:
>
> Exuse me if I'm posting questions that have been asked before, but I'm
> running 2.5.7 FVWM with the themes, and I'd really like to get transparent
> Xterms and menus (that is, I'd like the text to appear as if it were on
> the root window where my background picture is displayed).
>
> I understand the layout for Themes, as I've begun to piece together my own
> from the ones available. I tracked down an asortment of info regarding
> Colorsets, ParentalRelativity, fvwm-root -d, and so on, but I just can't
> put the pieces together.

1) Run "aterm -tr" or other terminals like gnome-terminal and Eterm that
support transparency (see their man pages), and set the root using
fvwm-root -r (or fvwm-themes-root in pre-0.7.0).

The Themes FAQ may help to make such transparent terminal to be your
default terminal.

2) Several days ago I answered how to make transparent menus in 2.5.x.
Here are the lines again (try in FvwmConsole first):

  Colorset 5 RootTransparent buffer, Tint rgb:00/30/30 20, bg average, bgTint rgb:00/30/30 20
  MenuStyle * MenuColorset 5 # this line is redundand for fvwm-themes

If you have problems with creating your own theme, ask on the fvwm-themes
lists.

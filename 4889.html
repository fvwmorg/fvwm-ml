--- Dan Espen <dane_at_mk.telcordia.com> wrote:
> Christian MICHON <christian_michon_at_yahoo.fr> writes:
> > > Fvwm itself won't do that, you need to start one
> of the screen
> > > lockers, xscreensaver, xlock...
> > >
> > > You can do that in your .xinitrc.
> >
> > We use xautolock 2.0 here. Very stable.
> > .xinitrc calls it like this:
> > xautolock -time 10 -corners 0+-- -cornersize 10
> -cornerdelay 1 -cornerredelay
> > 10 &
> >
> > After 10 minutes, it autolocks. If you're lazy,
> like me, just pull the mouse
> > to the top right of the screen, and after 1 or 2
> seconds it'll lock too.
>
> For the keyboard oriented:
>
> In my .xinitrc:
>
> nice xscreensaver -timeout 30 -cycle 60 -lock-mode&
>
> In my .fvwm2rc:
>
> Key Scroll_Lock A N Exec xscreensaver-command
> -activate
>
> Locks after 30 minutes, changes every 60 minutes.
> The scroll lock key locks the display.

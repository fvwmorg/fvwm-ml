On Mon, May 26, 2003 at 09:31:22AM +0200, Dominik Vogt wrote:
> On Mon, May 26, 2003 at 01:31:51PM +0800, Hongzheng Wang wrote:
> > Hi. Here is the version information about my fvwm:
> >
> > fvwm 2.5.6 compiled on Feb 28 2003 at 06:47:05
> > with support for: ReadLine, Stroke, XPM, PNG, Shape, XShm, SM,
> > Bidi text, Xinerama, XRender, XFT, NLS
> >
> > I asked this quesion on gThumb maillist, and Some people said :
> >
> > if I remember well old versions of gThumb required your window manager
> > to support the _NET_WM_STATE_FULLSCREEN property and I dubt this is the
> > case for Fvwm.
>
> Fvwm fully supports _NET_WM_STATE_FULLSCREEN. I assume it does
> not work with gThumb because it sets _NET_WM_STATE_FULLSCREEN
> after its window is already mapped.
>

Ooops, In fact fvwm (2.5.x branch) does not support
_NET_WM_STATE_FULLSCREEN, I do not see how to implement this without a
new command or a new option to Maximize "fullscreen" which will remove
the decoration, maximize and possibly put the window into layer +2
(and keep in memory what is needed to restore the window). I've planed
to do this so fvwm claim to support _NET_WM_STATE_FULLSCREEN this is a
bug. Two possible fixes: add this fullscreen option to Maximize or
remove _NET_WM_STATE_FULLSCREEN from the list of the supported EWMH
stuff.

>>>>> "BM" == Brady Montz <bradym_at_cs.arizona.edu> writes:

BM> Let me try to rephrase the points I have poorly made:

BM> 1. I dearly wish that fvwm would decouple focus and warping.
BM> 2. I know at least three people, including myself, who have written patches
BM> to fvwm to do this.

I know... I just haven't had a chance to evaluate them yet. Or
rather, it wasn't high on my priority list, since I use ClickToFocus
(gasp!).

BM> 3. If you use my patch, which is included with FvwmIconMan, you get the
BM> exact icon manager behavior it seems that you want. On the
BM> setup I use, windows having focus are displayed differently in
BM> the manager, and by moving my cursor around in the manager, I
BM> can change the focus of the managed windows. No warping or
BM> raising. In addition, you could use my patch and not use my
BM> icon manager at all. No biggie.

BM> Three additional points:

BM> 4. I really don't like keeping this patch up to date.

Understandable.

BM> 5. I would like to discuss with somebody who understands the
BM> internals of fvwm better that I how important this restriction
BM> that only windows on the current desk can have focus. I get the
BM> impression that we have about 5 people here who are waiting for
BM> somebody else to do this. Let's write ourselves some code.

I've never really though about it since taking it over, probably
because I normally only use one desktop.

BM> 6. I don't want Chuck Hines to get the impression that I am
BM> harping on him. The guy does have a job after all, in addition
BM> to trying to keep all of us happy.

And I appreciate the consideration, especially since I may be somewhat
silent for the remainder of the day (having already spent way too much
time answering mail today).

BTW - I don't feel that you're harping on me, yet. ;^)

So, I concede that the warping should be decoupled from the focus
function, and I will do my best to remember to take care of it somehow
(via some variant of one of those patches) for the next beta release.

dennye_at_doc.crd.ge.com (Erik Denny) writes:

> I'm new to the list today, but I'll jump in feet first anyway.
>
> First, let me say I love fvwm. Once I get a little more into the
> customization of it, it'll be perfect.
>
> The major problem I have is: I have a Sun Sparc with 2 heads. I
> looked through the mailing list archives, but can only find vague
> references to running 2 monitors.
>
> Can someone, publically or privately, advise me as to how to set up
> a dual-headed monster?
>
> I am running 1.2x fvwm on Solaris 2.5.1.

Your X server (X11R6?) should manage all of your screens when it
starts. If it doesn't you might have to use the -dev argument or the
XDEVICE environment variable. fvwm obtains the number of screens from
the X server and then starts window managers on each of those screens.

If I remember correctly, fvwm 1.2x worked with on multiple screens
without any special configuration.

chuck_hines> CurrentDesk - As expected
chuck_hines> CurrentPage - This page, this desk

Why not getting more simple way:

CurrentDesk - to specify 'current desk', as expected.
                (ofcourse, do not consider page)
CurrentPage - to specify 'current page', as expected.
                (ofcourse, do not consider desk)

Is it not enough ? There are only TWO dimension, Desk and Page, but
why we need THREE flags ?

If my knowledge about Next/Prev/None implementation is right, and THIS
MY 'CurrentPage/CurrentDesk' DESIGN PROPOSAL is implemented, we can say

        Next [CurrentDesk,CurrentPage] whatever

to specify "search window which is in current desk and is in current
page, and if found, execute whatever (maybe user-defined function)".

I think it's very simple and strong, isn't it ? Or my misunderstanding ?

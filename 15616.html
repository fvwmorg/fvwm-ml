I just caught up on my FVWM list mail... more of a response than I expected!
Thanks to all who sent me information; specifically, Albrecht Kadlec, Michael
Tiefenback, and Alexander Sorg, who all pointed out the solution to my
button-placement problem. Since FvwmButtons arranges buttons left-to-right,
then top-to-bottom, a 2-row button at the end of my setup always appeared as a
single-row button in the bottom row. With that button placed at the end of the
first row of buttons, however, the other buttons arrange themselves correctly.
Here's my revised setup:

*FvwmButtonsFore DEFAULT_FOREGROUND
*FvwmButtonsBack DEFAULT_BACKGROUND
*FvwmButtonsGeometry 650x40+0-0
*FvwmButtonsRows 2
*FvwmButtonsFont DEFAULT_TITLE_FONT
*FvwmButtons(1x2) FVWM xlogo16.xpm menu main-menu
*FvwmButtons(5x2) - - swallow "xloadFvwm" exec xload -name
xloadFvwm &
*FvwmButtons Main - desk 0 0
*FvwmButtons WebSrv - desk 0 1
*FvwmButtons Browse1 - desk 0 2
*FvwmButtons Browse2 - desk 0 3
*FvwmButtons Notes - desk 0 4
*FvwmButtons Post-Its - desk 0 5
# this was at the end of the list
*FvwmButtons(1x2) - - swallow "xclockFvwm" exec xclock
-name xclockFvwm &
*FvwmButtons WebLog - desk 0 6
*FvwmButtons PrxyLog - desk 0 7
*FvwmButtons Misc1 - desk 0 8
*FvwmButtons Misc2 - desk 0 9
*FvwmButtons Misc3 - desk 0 10
*FvwmButtons Mail - desk 0 11
# xclock button used to be here

Just to check, I looked at the manual page; there's no mention of FvwmButtons'
method for arranging things. Yet another addition/correction for the man pages,
I guess -- sorry, Chuck. 1/2 :-)


As for my menu problems, I goofed. In my original message, I said I used
fvwm-style menus (click-to-display). In fact, I use mwm-style menus, which
automatically appear as the pointer enters them. I've switched to fvwm-style
menus for the time being, but that doesn't really eliminate the problem: a
submenu should appear to the left of its parent when there's insufficient room
to the right of the parent.

Hi.

I'm a long-time FvwmTaskBar enthusiast. It is the most efficient
desktop-clutter-reduction tool that I have encountered to date.

I have a suggestion for a feature, based on one of the few things
in GNOME that I actually like. The Gnome Tasklist allows "task
grouping", in which multiple instances of a program are grouped in the
same taskbar icon. Clicking on that icon pops up a menu showing
the titlebar entry of all instances, allowing one to choose any one
of them.

This effects an enormous space saving on my typical desktop. I find
that just being able to group all terminals together and all edit
windows together allows me to keep a tasklist that monitors all clients
in all desktops using no more than 8-10 icons. This makes the icons
readable and usable, while requiring only two lines of icons in the
tasklist.

In my opinion, this would be a great addition to FvwmTaskBar.

On Wed, Dec 23, 1998 at 08:14:56AM -0500, clinee_at_nnr.dowjones.com wrote:
> There is like no one in this week, being so close to christmas, so I'm
> bored and trying to configure fvwm more to my liking. I run the newest
> ver. on Solaris 2.5.1 on My sparc 5. My video card kinda blows (CG6
> with 1M video memory), so I'm limited as to how much I can run. Anyway,
> quick question for everyone: How can I just change the window border
> colors from what I guess is 'stock' (its grey non in focus, and in-focus
> is this weird pink sort of color). I know if I made pixmaps I could do
> it, but I just want to change them to standard (albeit different) rgb
> colors. Doable?

Of course. For the focussed window you can use

  HilightColors <textcolour> <backbroundcolour>

For all other windows it can be defined per window:

  Style "*" ForeColor <forecolour>, BackColor <backcolor>

If you mean 2.1.5 with 'the newest ver.' you need not put
the asterisk in double quotes. You might want to read the
'Style' section in the manpage for more options.

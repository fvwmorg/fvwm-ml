Its actually a rather simple problem, your gcc is built for solaris 2.5.1
and your are compiling on 2.6. The solution is simple, rebuild gcc for
2.6 or compile on 2.5.1 and then copy it to your 2.6 machine. This is not
an fvwm problem but a compiler problem. I'm supprised this is the first
you noticed it, as soon as I upgraded to 2.6 I couldn't get a thing to
build with the old gcc.

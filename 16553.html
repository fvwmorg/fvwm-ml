In the April 1996 issue of 'Sys Admin' (Volume 5, Number 4), there
is an article about fvwm entitled "Customizing X Window Sessions with fvwm."
It is written by Richard L. Brownrigg.

The article mentions Robert Nation and Charles Hines. It talks about the
various features of fvwm. The general feeling in the article is that
fvwm is a very useful window manager.

The only thing remotely negative said about fvwm was said in an insert panel.
This panel states that the desktop concept is not as useful as it could
be. He (Richard?) says that having different desktops use different
configurations, and different colormaps, would make it more usefull. He
says that as it stands, virtual desks add little to the virtual page concept.

Overall, the article was very positive.

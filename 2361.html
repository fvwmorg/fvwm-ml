On 17 Apr 2001 10:25:41 -0300, Christian Lyra wrote:
>
> Maybe I'm coming too late :-), but I have a tip for those of you who are
> looking for the standart Alt+Tab behavior in Fvwm. I mean switching between
> the various running programs....
>
> This line on .fvwm2rc do the trick. Note that the alt+tab are tied to
> winlist but you can override this (at least with the last beta version...
> the one that I'm using :-) ).
>
> Key Tab A M Next [*] Focus

I myself prefer the default behaviour of Alt-Tab in 2.3.x, because it does
not immediately jump to a random (almost) window, like in your example. :)

> You can go further and use shift+alt+tab to switching in the reverse order:
>
> key Tab A SM Prev [*] Focus
>
> But you can do some other tricks too... the [*] are used to define what
> Styles the Fvwm will look for when switching programs, so you can use keys to
> circulate between a group of windows. Here I use Crtl+tab to circulate
> between my E-terms:
>
> key Tab A C Next [Eterm*] Focus
>
>
> May I sugest to include this on the FAQ?

I think that if we are going to add something like this to FAQ we should
use round brackets, not obsolete square brackets. Also most of users would
probably want to call a user function here, like:

  Next (*, !Iconic, CurrentDesk) SelectWindow

so they would do more useful things in SelectWindow than just focusing,
for example, wrapping a cursor to a window center, raising, unshading.

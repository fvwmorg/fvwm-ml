On Mon, Nov 04, 2002 at 11:06:18PM -0500, Daniel Barclay wrote:
> Also, does anyone have any pointers to GNOME documentation regarding
> how to avoid wasting so much screen real estate (the two-row bar at the
> bottom plus the menu bar at the top of the screen)?

On my system I use only one gnome panel, at the top of my screen. I have its
size set to "Tiny (24 pixels)". In it I have icons to launch some common
applications along with the gnome "Main Menu" icon, a mail check applet (set to
display either "No mail" or "x messages"), a gnomeicu (ICQ clone) applet, a
mixer applet, and a clock. I find that this gives me everything that I need
without wasting too much space. I'm running in 1024x768.

Have a look at http://www.humbug.net/shot1.png for an example (~100k). I use
gnome 1.4 but I don't imagine it would be too drastically different in gnome 2.

> >> Another related thing: In CreatingNewPages it says that the WikiNames for
> >> new subsections should be e.g. Tips/CenterWindow instead of CenterWindow.
> >> Doesn't this cause inconsistencies when rearranging sections as there seems
> >> to be no action in the Wiki for renaming pages?
> >
> > we thought about this. and this is why our subpages structure is not
> > very deep. the subpages hirachy is independent from the hirachy on the
> > frontpage. Tips/CenterWindow is a tip, and always will be a tip, and so

>
> But i have alwsy to refer to it as Tips/CenterWindow or need i just to
> use CenterWindow? Maybe in the future there will be a lot of tips/* and
> i don't really want to see them all on every tips-page. And then again
> somebody will have to rename them on your machine to cut the piece
> section in smaller, well defined pieces... I don't really got the
> advantage of this whole subpage thing?!

Here is the advantage. Does CenterWindow page describe an fvwm command
(there were thoughts to add it), your new shell script, your config
snippet or some application? This can't be guessed from the name, so
such page name seems to be bad. Tips/CenterWindow name is intuitive.
Use ["Tips/CenterWindow"] syntax in your other pages to refer to it.

Think also about pages like Instalation/GNOME (compile time headers),
Integration/GNOME (run time), Scripts/GNOMEMenus (with user script
creating such menus attached), Tips/GNOMEMenus (using FvwmGtk or other
distributed modules or scripts). Any such name conflicts should be
reduced from the start.

I don't think this mailing list is good for discussing every small detail
of Wiki, Probably using irc or Wiki itself to leave your ideas, comments,
or questions is more appropriate.

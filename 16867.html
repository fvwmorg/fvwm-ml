>
> GNU autoconf decides libraries and files are exists. Suppose directories
> /usr/local/X11R5 and /usr/local/X11R6 are both exist, GNU autoconf can
> decide "which version of X to use" ? With imake, you can easily decide
> with using which imake (/usr/local/X11R5/bin or /usr/local/X11R6/bin)
> is suitable.

This is why configure takes the options --x-includes=DIR and
--x-libraries=DIR so that you can manually override configure. Yes,
autoconf/configure is quite flexible. And in the above example, configure
would choose the imake that comes first in your path, so if this is not
what you want, then just override it with the command-line options to
configure.
